Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

This case study presents a realistic fictional scenario of UPI-based online payment system fraud targeting a first-year college student in India. Nikhil, a first-year B.Tech student staying in a hostel, received an SMS claiming to be from his food ordering application, claiming he was eligible for a refund since he had made a “failed order.” After a few minutes, he received a WhatsApp message from an unknown sender whose profile picture was the logo of the company, asking him to process the refund through UPI. The scammer claimed that Rohan would get an instant refund if he processed some “verification steps.” He was first asked to share the last four digits of his bank account and download a remote access application “to resolve the issue quickly.” The scammer then sent UPI-based “collect request” notifications and asked Rohan to approve them, claiming they were necessary to “reverse” the amount. In a matter of minutes, after approving multiple requests, several transactions were made, silently deducting money from   Nikhil's account. Nikhil lost most of the money in his savings bank account, which he used for daily expenses. He felt ashamed and scared of telling his parents. Moreover, his belief in digital transactions came down significantly. Looking back, Nikhil should not have revealed any of the details, should not have allowed any unknown app on his phone, and should not have accepted any collect requests from strangers. He should have contacted the official customer care from within the app, informed his bank, and registered a complaint through the National Cyber Crime Portal and helpline 1930.
