Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings and missed opportunities. For example, a student once sent an email to a professor requesting an assignment extension but used informal language, no subject line, and did not clearly explain the reason. As a result, the professor ignored the email, assuming it was not important or genuine, and the student lost marks due to late submission. This situation could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette, such as writing a clear subject line, using a respectful greeting, and explaining the request politely and concisely. Additionally, proofreading the message before sending would have improved clarity and professionalism. Effective digital communication ensures that the message is understood correctly and creates a positive impression, which is especially important in academic and professional environments.
